5.2 Implementation-defined Features

The PL/I language features listed below are termed IMPLEMENTATION-DEFINED : their specification is regarded as completing the definition of the language for a particular implementation. A brief description of each feature is given, with references in parentheses to the sections of this document where further details can be found.

1. The actual restrictions imposed, if any, under the provisions for restrictions on the size of constructs (item 1 of 1.5.1.2) or in addressing (items 1, 2 and 3 of 1.5.1.3).

2. Whether or not case mapping is performed on &identifier$s (Step 6.1 of 4.3.2).

3. The mapping functions for <external-name>s and <identifier>s to <environment-name>s (4.8.2).

4. Determination of the edataset-list> passed to the INTERPRET operation (1.7.3.3).

5. Determination of the Qentry-value, passed to the INTERPRET operation (1.7.3.3).

6. ENVIRONMENT attribute and option syntax (2.4.11.4) and semantics (Chapter 8 and item 1 in 1.5.1.8).

7. OPTIONS attribute and option syntax (2.4.11.4) and semantics.

8. Consistency requirements for ENVIRONMENT and OPTIONS attributes in EXTERNAL declarations(4.8.1), and for OPTIONS attributes in ENTRY references (6.4.7.1.1).

9. The form of the <text-name$ in the INCLUDE construct (2.5.6).

10. Interpretation of UNSPEC pseudo-variable (7.6.4.6).

11. Result of UNSPEC built-in function (9.5.4.81).

12. Length of string returned by TIME built-in function (9.5.4.77) and of the DATETIME built-in function (9.5.4.21).

13. Extralingual characters in data character set (2.5.5 and 2.6.2).

15. The external hardware representations of the characters specified by *symbol>, i.e., the set of characters in which PL/I programs are written (2.6).

16. Representation of currency symbol and digit and sign symbols (9.6.2.2).

17. Default precisions of arithmetic data (4.553).

18. Maximum <number-of-digits> for each combination of <base> and <scale>(4.6.3.5,9.2.3.2,9.6.1.9, and elsewhere).

19. Precision of integer-type (9.2.2).

20. Accuracy and range of floating-point results of expressions and built-in functions (1.6.3.7.2,9.2.4, and elsewhere).

21. Accuracy and range of ROUND built-in function with floating-point argument (954.61).

22. Results of numeric conversions (9.6.1.2, 9.6.1.3, and 9.6.1.6).
